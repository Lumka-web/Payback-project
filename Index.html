Daddy Issues: A Field Guide to Surviving With a Broken Default Settings Father

  • Face It:Your Dad Wasn't the Blueprint.He wasn't even IKEA instructions,at least those come with diagrams. He was more like the random Allen key: useless, yet somehow you keep tripping over him.
  • Stop Romanticizing the Trauma “He made me strong.” No, he made you overthink every text message and clap when men do the bare minimum. Strength didn't come from him; it came from you realizing you can't raise yourself and your partner at the same time.
  • Notice the Patterns Before You Marry Them.If he looks like your dad, argues like your dad, and disappears emotionally like your dad—congrats, you're not dating a man, you're speedrunning your childhood. Put him back in the recycling bin where he belongs.
  • Therapy is expensive. Gym memberships? Slightly less expensive. Punch a bag, run like you're escaping family dinners, or do push-ups every time you think “maybe he'll change.” Spoiler:he won't.
  • Your dad thinks he can call, guilt-trip, or passive-aggressively sabotage you? Block. Mute. Ghost. You're not being disrespectful; you're just unsubscribing from a toxic subscription you never signed up for.
  • Your actual dad didn't set the bar high. You can literally choose anyone else. A bus driver who smiles at you = more paternal effort. A cartoon lion who dies in the first act? Still a better father figure.
  • You don't have daddy issues,you're just running a beta version of life with buggy coding. The fix? Stop waiting for an update from the manufacturer. He's discontinued.

Your dad might've fumbled being a father, but at least he gave you premium-grade character development. He's the villain, but you? You're the sarcastic anti-hero with main character trauma.
